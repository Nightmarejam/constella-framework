# Governance Cadence

The Governance Cadence is the **published, predictable, loose, multi-zone rhythm** the polity's recurring
functions run on — so participants can *phase-lock* to it and anticipate cheaply, instead of paying
constant-vigilance costs or being caught reactive. It is not a new policy; it is the **clock** the existing
policies run on.

## Why this exists (three-layer grounding)

- **Harmony (the principle).** Harmony's **Phase Flip** is the transition where intake (Yang) exchanges into
  return (Yin). The Cadence is the *rhythm* at which that flip recurs at the civic scale — the beat of
  contribution → accountability → rest → contribution. A polity with no shared rhythm cannot phase-flip
  coherently; it thrashes.
- **ALife (the receipts).** Two confirmed words back the design directly (see
  [alife_evidence_mapping.md](alife_evidence_mapping.md)):
  - **`entrainment`** — under a *predictable* rhythm, agents phase-lock and anticipate at low cost; the
    lock is **voluntary** (a strategy that pays off, never a coercion) and it needs a **shared/broadcast**
    reference (private clocks drift). This is *why the cadence is loose and published, not mandatory and
    private.*
  - **`niche-maintains-diversity`** — a single *uniform* environment **converges** (one strategy takes
    over); *multiple niches with independent capacity* **maintain** diversity. Applied to time: **one
    universal clock homogenizes lifestyles (the rat race); multiple zone-clocks maintain lifestyle
    diversity.** This is *why the cadence is multi-zone.*
- **The heart (the value — asserted).** The clock should support *any and all lifestyles*, not force one
  tempo on everyone. Most of it should be loose enough that a person who blows past it — owing nothing — is
  not punished. The system's purpose is to give people back *time*, not to schedule it away.

## Core structure: two tiers + zones + a protected floor

1. **Hard beats (few, mandatory).** The genuinely load-bearing recurring functions that *must* happen on
   schedule — e.g. safety reviews, floor-funding cycles, ratification windows. These are consequential and
   reliable *because entrainment only pays off when the rhythm actually matters.* Keep them **as few as
   integrity allows.**
2. **Offered beats (many, voluntary — no penalty).** A wide field of *available* rhythms a participant can
   lock onto to make life cheaper and more anticipable — or ignore, with **no penalty.** (The tax-form you
   don't owe on is an offered beat, not a hard one.) This tier is where the clock is loose.
3. **Per-zone clocks (temporal/lifestyle niches).** Different zones run their own offered rhythms: a
   data-center/operational zone on a fast beat, a holistic/retreat zone on a slow seasonal one. Participants
   **migrate between zones** — toward a desired workload, or out to rest — the way agents settle into
   matching niches. (This is the `niche-maintains-diversity` receipt made civic.)
4. **The fallow constraint.** No beat — hard *or* offered — may be placed inside a participant's
   [Temporal Floor](temporal_floor.md). The Cadence sets the beats; the floor guarantees the silence
   between them. *This constraint is what keeps the whole clock honest.*

## Mechanism notes

- **Published, not private.** The cadence is a shared public calendar (the *nurture* calibration in
  `entrainment`) — a coherent picture of what society expects and when. Participants re-sync to the
  published schedule; they are not asked to each keep a private clock that drifts.
- **Consequential ⇒ hard; inconsequential ⇒ drop or make offered.** A "hard" beat that no one needs to
  prepare for confers no benefit and should be demoted. Vigilance is expensive; only spend it on beats that
  earn it.
- **Zone autonomy.** Zones set their own *offered* rhythms freely; only the small set of *hard* beats is
  shared across all zones.

## Boundaries (what this does NOT claim)

- The **structure** — loose two-tier, published, multi-zone, floor-protected — is receipt-backed
  (`entrainment`, `niche-maintains-diversity`). **Which** functions are hard vs offered, and **what** the
  zones are, are governance/values choices, not sandbox results.
- The specific lifestyle zones (data cities, holistic-learning retreats, etc.) are **pilot/aspirational**
  content — the sandbox backs *that niches maintain diversity*, not *which* niches a society should build.
