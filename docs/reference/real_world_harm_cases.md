# Real-world harm & resolution cases (seed set)

*The concrete start of the comparative deep-dive (banked in the roadmap). Real cases recorded in the
[Observability Layer](../governance/observability_layer.md)'s observation shape, to begin backing the
[Harm & Repair](../governance/harm_and_repair.md) gradient with **human** receipts instead of only
sandbox ones — the "real life examples of court cases or resolutions" the boundaries were said to need.*

**Tier: reference / contested.** Specifics (dates, counts, rates) are recorded at the level of general
confidence and **flagged `[verify]`** where a primary source should confirm them before any figure is
treated as `stable`. Nothing here is policy. *Observe, don't judge* — these describe what was done and what
followed, not verdicts.

## Case 1 — South Africa: Truth and Reconciliation Commission (1995–2002)

- **condition:** a nation transitioning out of apartheid chose a *restorative* reckoning over mass
  prosecution — public truth-telling with conditional amnesty rather than victors' trials.
- **measures:** amnesty granted in exchange for *full disclosure* of politically-motivated acts; on the
  order of thousands of amnesty applications, a minority granted `[verify counts]`; public victim hearings.
- **context:** the alternative — Nuremberg-style trials — risked reigniting conflict in a fragile
  transition; the wager was that *truth surfaced* would do more than *punishment imposed.*
- **scope:** population (national process).
- **provenance:** Promotion of National Unity and Reconciliation Act (1995); chaired by Desmond Tutu
  `[verify specifics against the TRC final report]`.
- **resolution (how it was delegated):** a standing commission, case by case, amnesty conditioned on truth
  — **process pre-decided, outcomes not.** Widely credited with averting a revenge cycle; also criticized
  for letting perpetrators avoid accountability and for thin reparations `[verify]`.
- **claim label:** contested.
- **maps to:** harm-gradient **band 4** (extreme harm → *process, not pre-set outcome*) and band 1
  (making historical harm *observable* so it can be owned).

## Case 2 — Norway: the rehabilitation / "normality" model (Halden and the wider system)

- **condition:** a penal system built on *reintegration* rather than punishment — prison conditions kept
  close to ordinary life, focus on skills, dignity, and return to society.
- **measures:** reported 2-year recidivism around ~20%, low relative to punitive systems `[verify — figure
  is widely cited but ranges by definition/window]`.
- **context:** an explicit bet that treating the incarcerated as future neighbors *lowers* future harm.
- **scope:** population (system-level), individual (each sentence).
- **provenance:** Norwegian Correctional Service "normality principle" `[verify primary source]`.
- **resolution:** the person is *relocated to a rehabilitative setting, self-first, with a path back to
  standing* — not lost. This is the real-world shape of the harm-gradient's **healing-niche** refinement.
- **claim label:** contested.
- **maps to:** band 3 (other-regarding harm → reintegration over exclusion); a direct human analogue of
  the sandbox's `reintegration-over-exclusion`.

## Case 3 — United States/Mexico: the Bracero Program (1942–1964)

- **condition:** a bilateral guest-worker program that brought millions of Mexican laborers to U.S.
  agriculture; widespread documented exploitation — wage withholding, poor conditions, few protections.
- **measures:** roughly 4–5 million contracts over its span `[verify]`; ~10% of wages routinely *withheld*
  as forced "savings" that many workers never received back `[verify]`.
- **context:** *inherited harm* — the cost fell on workers and cascaded to their descendants (a personal
  example for this project's author); **not the fault of those who carry it.**
- **scope:** population (the program), family (generational).
- **provenance:** the 1942 bilateral agreement and later litigation records `[verify]`.
- **resolution (delegated, decades late):** class-action lawsuits in the 2000s produced a partial
  settlement (reported ~2008–2009, a few thousand dollars per eligible surviving worker) `[verify]` — a
  small, late, *observability-and-repair* outcome rather than blame.
- **claim label:** contested.
- **maps to:** band 1 (inherited/historical harm → make it *legible*, let it be *owned*, meet it with a
  way forward).

## What this seed shows (early pattern, not yet a receipt)

Across three very different societies, the harm-gradient's shape recurs: **restorative/reintegrative
responses aimed at a way-forward, process pre-decided at the extreme, and historical harm resolved (when
at all) through observability + repair rather than blame.** That *rhymes* with the sandbox receipts
(`reintegration-over-exclusion`, `niche-maintains-diversity`) — but it is not yet confirmed: it needs many
more cases, primary sources, and the `[verify]` flags cleared before any boundary is promoted past
`contested`. This file is where that accumulation begins.
