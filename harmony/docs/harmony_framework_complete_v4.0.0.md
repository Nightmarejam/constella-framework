## 1. Framework Overview

The Harmony Framework describes how the human body processes gravitational load through distributed resonance, phase-exchange nodes, and stabilizing flows. It comprises three modules:

| Module | Function | Phase Flip Location |
|--------|----------|---------------------|
| **Head** | First-stage gain: sensory intake, orientation | Jaw → Hyoid → C1 |
| **Feet** | Second-stage gain: grounding, propulsion | Subtalar → Midfoot → Windlass |
| **Hands** | Micro-tuning: precision adjustment, lateral interface | Proximal Carpals → Midcarpal → CMC |

## 2. Three-Layer Structure

All documents follow a consistent three-layer format:

1. **Anatomical Reality** — Observable structures, measurable relationships, established biomechanics
2. **Functional Interpretation** — How structures interact under load, movement patterns, clinical correlates
3. **Symbolic/Metaphorical** — Yang/Yin mapping, resonance language, Constella parallels

This layering ensures claims remain falsifiable at Layer 1, clinically useful at Layer 2, and philosophically coherent at Layer 3.

## 3. Canonical Definitions

### Phase Flip
A biomechanical transition point where anterior-descending fascial tension exchanges with posterior-ascending stabilization. Involves:
- **Reciprocal inhibition** between antagonist muscle groups
- **Fascial tension exchange** across anatomical planes
- **Autonomic shift** in regional tone

### Resonance
A stable oscillatory relationship among tissues, pressures, and gravitational load that allows efficient distribution of forces without collapse or rigidity.

### Yang (Intake)
Directional flow associated with sensory reception, forward orientation, and load acceptance. Anatomically correlates with anterior and descending pathways.

### Yin (Return)
Stabilizing flow associated with grounding, posterior support, and proprioceptive feedback. Anatomically correlates with posterior and ascending pathways.

### Micro-buoyancy
The cranio-cervical system's continuous micro-movements that create dynamic equilibrium at the atlanto-occipital junction.

### Vertical Fascial Chain
The continuous myofascial pathway: plantar fascia → Achilles tendon → hamstrings → sacrotuberous ligament → thoracolumbar fascia → nuchal ligament → occiput.

## 4. Head Module Document v2.1

### Layer 1: Anatomical Reality

**Cranial Vault**
- 22 bones (8 cranial, 14 facial) joined by sutures permitting micro-movement
- Falx cerebri and tentorium cerebelli create internal dural partitions
- Cerebrospinal fluid provides hydraulic cushioning (~150ml total volume)

**Eyes**
- Six extraocular muscles per eye coordinate gaze
- Direct neural connection to vestibular nuclei via medial longitudinal fasciculus
- Orbital fascia continuous with periosteum and dural attachments

**Vestibular Apparatus**
- Three semicircular canals (anterior, posterior, lateral) detect angular acceleration
- Otolith organs (utricle, saccule) detect linear acceleration and head position relative to gravity
- Vestibular nuclei integrate with cervical proprioceptors and visual input

**Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ)**
- Bicondylar synovial joint with articular disc
- Masticatory muscles: masseter, temporalis, medial/lateral pterygoids
- Dense innervation from trigeminal nerve (V3)

**Mandible**
- Largest, strongest facial bone
- Attachment site for suprahyoid muscles (mylohyoid, geniohyoid, digastric)
- Movement couples with cervical spine via fascial and muscular connections

**Tongue**
- Eight muscles (4 intrinsic, 4 extrinsic)
- Hyoglossus, genioglossus, styloglossus, palatoglossus connect to hyoid, mandible, styloid, palate
- Innervation: hypoglossal (XII) for motor, trigeminal/facial/glossopharyngeal for sensory

**Hyoid Bone**
- Only bone not articulating with another bone
- Suspended by suprahyoid muscles (above) and infrahyoid muscles (below)
- Serves as mobile anchor for tongue, pharynx, and larynx

**Atlanto-Occipital Junction (C0-C1)**
- Permits ~25° flexion/extension (nodding)
- Rectus capitis posterior major/minor and obliquus capitis superior/inferior provide fine control
- Suboccipital triangle contains vertebral artery and C1 spinal nerve

### Layer 2: Functional Interpretation

**Yang Intake Flow**
Eyes → Vestibular system → Cranial vault

Visual orientation and vestibular signaling establish directional intent. The cranial vault distributes incoming sensory load across dural membranes and CSF, creating a hydraulic intake buffer.

**Yin Return Flow**
Jaw → Tongue → Hyoid → C0-C1

Jaw position modulates cervical muscle tone via trigeminocervical reflex. Tongue posture affects hyoid position, which influences infrahyoid strap muscles connecting to sternum and scapula. The C0-C1 junction serves as the final relay point where cranial input transitions to spinal output.

**Phase Flip Mechanism**
At the jaw-hyoid-C1 junction:
1. Anterior tension (suprahyoid, anterior cervical) yields
2. Posterior tension (suboccipital, posterior cervical) engages
3. Load transfers from cranial intake to spinal distribution

**Micro-buoyancy**
The atlanto-occipital junction maintains continuous micro-oscillations that prevent static loading. This "float" depends on:
- Balanced suboccipital muscle tone
- Adequate CSF pressure
- Proper hyoid suspension

### Layer 3: Symbolic/Metaphorical

**Yang as Sensory Intake**
The eyes and vestibular system represent the organism's forward-facing, world-engaging orientation. This is "receiving" mode — gathering information about space, movement, and threat/opportunity.

**Yin as Stabilizing Return**
The jaw-tongue-hyoid complex represents the grounding response. Clenching anchors, swallowing integrates, and the hyoid's suspension creates a damping system that prevents over-reaction to sensory input.

**Phase Flip as Integration Point**
The jaw-hyoid-C1 region is where "intake" becomes "response." In Constella terms, this parallels the Penumbra Accord — where external signal (dissent, feedback) transforms into internal action (governance response) without losing coherence.

**Micro-buoyancy as Dynamic Stability**
The continuous float at C0-C1 represents the Universal Civic Floor — a baseline state that prevents total collapse while allowing adaptive movement.

### Head Module Testable Predictions

1. Jaw position changes will produce measurable shifts in posterior cervical EMG within 500ms
2. Tongue posture correlates with C1/C2 micro-movement detectable via ultrasound
3. Visual orientation tasks increase neck proprioceptive error more than non-visual tasks
4. Hydration state affects cranial micro-buoyancy frequency (measurable via specialized accelerometry)

## 6. Feet Module Document v1.0

### Layer 1: Anatomical Reality

**Bone Architecture (26 bones)**

*Hindfoot*
- Calcaneus: largest tarsal, receives Achilles tendon insertion
- Talus: articulates with tibia/fibula above, calcaneus below, navicular forward

*Midfoot*
- Navicular: keystone of medial arch
- Cuboid: lateral column support
- Cuneiforms (3): medial, intermediate, lateral — bridge midfoot to forefoot

*Forefoot*
- Metatarsals (5): long bones forming ball of foot
- Phalanges (14): toe bones, 2 for hallux, 3 for each lesser toe

**Plantar Muscle Layers (4 layers, superficial to deep)**

1. Abductor hallucis, flexor digitorum brevis, abductor digiti minimi
2. Quadratus plantae, lumbricals (4)
3. Flexor hallucis brevis, adductor hallucis, flexor digiti minimi brevis
4. Plantar interossei (3), dorsal interossei (4)

**Key Soft Tissues**

- Plantar fascia: thick aponeurosis from calcaneus to proximal phalanges
- Achilles tendon: gastrocnemius/soleus insertion on calcaneus
- Tibialis posterior tendon: primary dynamic arch support, inserts navicular
- Spring ligament (plantar calcaneonavicular): supports talar head

**Sensory Density**
- ~7,000+ nerve endings per cm² in plantar surface
- Mechanoreceptors: Meissner corpuscles, Pacinian corpuscles, Merkel discs, Ruffini endings

### Layer 2: Functional Interpretation

**Load Reception (Yang)**
Descending load enters via tibia → talus → distributes through arch system to tripod contact points (heel, 1st metatarsal head, 5th metatarsal head).

**Sensory Return (Yin)**
Plantar mechanoreceptors → plantar intrinsic muscle activation → Achilles/posterior chain → ascending proprioceptive signal to spine and vestibular integration.

**Phase Flip Mechanism**
At the subtalar-midfoot complex:

| Phase | State | Structural Behavior |
|-------|-------|---------------------|
| Yield (pronation) | Subtalar eversion, midfoot unlock | Arch lowers, foot adapts to terrain |
| Stiffen (supination) | Subtalar inversion, midfoot lock | Arch rises, foot becomes rigid lever for propulsion |

**Windlass Mechanism**
Toe dorsiflexion (especially hallux MTP) tensions plantar fascia → arch height increases → foot stiffens for push-off. This is the propulsive phase flip trigger.

**Vertical Fascial Chain**
The anatomical substrate for cross-system predictions:

Plantar fascia → Achilles tendon → Triceps surae → Hamstrings → Sacrotuberous ligament → Thoracolumbar fascia → Erector spinae → Nuchal ligament → Occiput

This chain allows jaw-hyoid-C1 changes to produce subtalar-midfoot changes within 2-3 gait cycles (Anchor Claim #1).

### Layer 3: Symbolic/Metaphorical

**Feet as Grounding Interface**
Where the body meets earth. The feet negotiate the boundary between self and environment, adapting to terrain while maintaining structural integrity.

**Yang as Load Acceptance**
The descending gravitational load is "received" through the arch system — an active acceptance, not passive collapse.

**Yin as Ground Feedback**
The dense sensory field of the plantar surface represents continuous dialogue with the earth. This information rises through the posterior chain to inform the entire system.

**Phase Flip as Gait Transition**
The pronation-supination cycle mirrors the Constella governance cycle: periods of openness/adaptation (pronation) followed by periods of decisive action (supination).

### Feet Module Testable Predictions

1. Arch compliance responds to head alignment changes (measurable via plantar pressure mapping)
2. Phase-flip timing correlates with propulsion efficiency (force plate analysis)
3. Tongue posture modifies center-of-pressure during standing (posturography)
4. Fascial hydration state modifies windlass mechanism efficiency
5. **Anchor Claim #1**: Jaw-hyoid-C1 interventions produce measurable subtalar-midfoot changes within 2-3 gait cycles

## 8. Hands Module Document v1.1

### Layer 1: Anatomical Reality

**Bone Architecture (27 bones per hand)**

*Carpals (8)*
- Proximal row: scaphoid, lunate, triquetrum, pisiform
- Distal row: trapezium, trapezoid, capitate, hamate

*Metacarpals (5)*
- Numbered I-V from thumb to small finger
- Thumb metacarpal (I) has unique saddle joint at CMC

*Phalanges (14)*
- Thumb: 2 (proximal, distal)
- Fingers II-V: 3 each (proximal, middle, distal)

**Radial-Ulnar Polarity**

| Column | Bones/Digits | Function |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| Radial | Thumb, index, scaphoid, trapezium | Precision, manipulation, intent |
| Ulnar | Ring, small, triquetrum, hamate | Power grip, stabilization |

**Muscular Organization**
- Thenar eminence: thumb opposition, abduction, flexion
- Hypothenar eminence: small finger opposition, abduction, flexion
- Interossei: finger abduction/adduction, MCP flexion
- Lumbricals: MCP flexion with IP extension

**Sensory Density**
- Fingertip receptor density: ~2,500 mechanoreceptors per cm²
- Highest two-point discrimination in the body (~2mm at fingertips)

**Fascial Connections**
- Palmar aponeurosis → forearm flexor compartment fascia
- Forearm fascia → bicipital aponeurosis → pectoral fascia
- Pectoral fascia → cervical fascia → cranial base

### Layer 2: Functional Interpretation

**Yang Flow (Radial Column)**
Precision grip and fine manipulation. The thumb-index pinch represents the primary "intent" interface with the world. Load flows: fingertip contact → radial carpals (scaphoid, trapezium) → radius → proximal arm.

**Yin Flow (Ulnar Column)**
Stabilization and power grip. The ulnar digits and hypothenar mass anchor the hand during manipulation. Load flows: ulnar palm → ulnar carpals (triquetrum, hamate) → ulna → proximal arm.

**Phase Flip Mechanism (Multiplanar)**
Unlike the sagittal-dominant phase flips of head and feet, the hand phase flip operates across all three planes:

| Plane | Movement | Function |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Sagittal | Wrist flexion/extension | Gross positioning |
| Frontal | Radial/ulnar deviation | Fine lateral adjustment |
| Transverse | Pronation/supination | Orientation to task |

The carpal complex (especially the midcarpal joint) serves as the phase-flip zone where distal intent converts to proximal stabilization.

**Micro-Tuning Function**
The hands provide real-time adjustment to the vertical system. Small changes in grip, finger spread, or wrist position ripple through the fascial connections to influence shoulder, cervical, and ultimately cranial positioning.

### Layer 3: Symbolic/Metaphorical

**Hands as World Interface**
The hands are where intention meets matter. They execute the will of the organism upon the environment.

**Radial Column as Yang/Intent**
The precision grip (thumb-index) represents focused, directed action — the "doing" mode.

**Ulnar Column as Yin/Stability**
The power grip and ulnar anchor represent grounded support — enabling precision by providing a stable base.

**Micro-Tuning as Governance Adjustment**
In Constella terms, the hands represent civic gestures: voting, testimony, mutual aid. Small individual actions that, aggregated, tune the entire system.

### Hands Module Testable Predictions

1. Grip modulation influences cervical EMG patterns (especially upper trapezius, SCM)
2. Precision grip increases oculomotor stability during visual tracking tasks
3. Midcarpal positioning affects scapular rotation during reaching
4. Finger spreading reduces trapezius EMG within 10 seconds
5. **Hand → Foot**: Precision grip alters subtalar pronation angle within 1-2 gait cycles
6. **Hand → Head**: Sustained grip increases jaw EMG (masseter/temporalis) within 30 seconds

These predictions link the three modules via the vertical fascial chain and represent the framework's most testable claims.

### Head ↔ Feet (Anchor Claim #1)

**Prediction:** Jaw-hyoid-C1 interventions produce measurable subtalar-midfoot kinematic changes within 2-3 gait cycles.

**Mechanism:** Posterior fascial chain transmits tension changes from cranial base through thoracolumbar region to lower extremity.

**Measurement:**
- Intervention: Manual jaw repositioning or hyoid mobilization
- Outcome: Subtalar eversion/inversion angle via motion capture
- Timeline: Immediate (within 2-3 steps)

### Hand ↔ Head

**Prediction:** Sustained precision grip increases jaw muscle EMG within 30 seconds.

**Mechanism:** Fascial continuity from palmar aponeurosis through anterior arm line to cervical fascia creates tension coupling.

**Measurement:**
- Intervention: Sustained pinch grip at 50% max
- Outcome: Masseter/temporalis EMG amplitude
- Timeline: 30-60 seconds

### Hand ↔ Foot

**Prediction:** Precision grip alters subtalar pronation angle within 1-2 gait cycles.

**Mechanism:** Hand tension influences trunk rotation, which affects pelvic mechanics and lower extremity loading patterns.

**Measurement:**
- Intervention: Grip vs. no-grip during walking
- Outcome: Subtalar kinematics via motion capture
- Timeline: Immediate

### Bidirectional Validation

All predictions should be tested in both directions to confirm true coupling rather than unidirectional influence.
